## Thermodynamics Project: Combined Power Generation and Desalination System

This project presents a thermodynamic analysis of a **combined power generation and water desalination system**. The system integrates a **Rankine cycle** powered by methane combustion with a **thermal desalination unit**, aiming to utilize energy efficiently for both electricity production and freshwater generation.

### 🔹 System Overview

The system consists of two main subsystems:

1. **Power Generation Cycle (Rankine Cycle)**

   * A boiler uses methane as fuel to generate high-pressure steam.
   * The steam expands through a turbine to produce mechanical work.
   * The exhaust steam is condensed in a condenser.
   * A pump recirculates the working fluid back to the boiler.

2. **Desalination Unit**

   * Seawater undergoes a pressure drop through an expansion valve.
   * A separator divides the flow into vapor and liquid phases.
   * The vapor is condensed to produce **freshwater**.
   * A heat exchanger recovers thermal energy from the power cycle to improve efficiency.

### 🔹 Objective

The main goal of this project is to investigate the **interaction between power production and desalination performance**, particularly how operating conditions affect both outputs.

### 🔹 Parametric Study

A parametric analysis was performed by varying the **condenser pressure (10–50 kPa)** and evaluating its effect on:

* Net power output (W_net)
* Methane fuel consumption
* Freshwater production rate

### 🔹 Key Results

* Increasing condenser pressure leads to:

  * 🔻 Decrease in net power output
  * 🔻 Reduction in fuel consumption
  * 🔺 Increase in freshwater production

This behavior highlights a **trade-off between energy generation and desalination efficiency**, which is critical in designing integrated energy-water systems.

### 🔹 Conclusion

The study demonstrates that waste heat from power cycles can be effectively utilized for desalination. However, optimal system performance requires balancing between maximizing power output and freshwater production.
